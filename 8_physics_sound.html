Sound Basics
Key concepts about sound
📳
Vibration
Sound is produced by vibrating objects. No vibration = No sound
🌊
Sound Wave
Sound travels as longitudinal waves through a medium
📏
Frequency
Number of vibrations per second. Unit: Hertz (Hz)
📈
Amplitude
Maximum displacement. Larger amplitude = Louder sound
🎵
Pitch
How high or low a sound is. Depends on frequency
🔊
Loudness
How strong a sound feels. Depends on amplitude
Bell Jar Experiment

🔔
🟢 Air Present - Sound can be heard

Sound Travels Through...
Different mediums conduct sound differently
💨
Air (Gas)
Speed: ~343 m/s. Sound travels slowest in gases
💧
Water (Liquid)
Speed: ~1500 m/s. 4x faster than in air
🪨
Steel (Solid)
Speed: ~5000 m/s. Fastest in solids
🚫
Vacuum
Speed: 0 m/s. No sound at all!
👂
Human Hearing
Range: 20 Hz - 20,000 Hz. Below 20 Hz = Infrasound
🦇
Ultrasound
Above 20,000 Hz. Used by bats and in medical imaging
